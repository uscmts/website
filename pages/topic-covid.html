COVID-19 WORKING GROUP

"COVID-19 has presented new challenges across the entire Marine Transportation System (MTS) that require a coordinated approach to ensure the continued efficient operation of our national supply chain… [we are] committed to ensuring that the CMTS partnership is well-positioned to support enhanced collaboration through the COVID-19 Working Group."

-RDML Rich Timme Assistant Commandant for Prevention Policy, U.S. Coast Guard, Chair, CMTS Coordinating Board

Purpose

The COVID-19 Working Group (C-19 WG) serves as the CMTS forum for facilitating high-level interagency discussion, communication, and actions in support of the MTS under and after COVID-19.

Contact Us

MTS stakeholders are welcome to submit their recommendations and questions to the Covid-19 Working Group at C19WG@cmts.gov.

Value

Enhanced interagency discussion and communication regarding the impact on the MTS will ensure that Federal MTS partners are providing the best and most effective support to the MTS under and after the pandemic

Areas of Focus

  • Navigable Waterways
  • Physical and Information Infrastructure
  • Vessel Operations and Response Services
  • The Human Element and U.S. Arctic Communities
  • Facilitate interagency communications and stakeholder engagement
  • Review federal policies, regulatory practices and guidance impacting safe, effective and efficient operation of the MTS under and after the COVID-19 pandemic.
  • Review and provide comments on MTS industry developed COVID-19 Best Practices and Resources.
  • Share and improve resources for addressing U.S. merchant mariner mental health and safety.
  • Assess MTS performance under and after COVID-19 pandemic and develop lessons learned.

Activity and Milestones

  • Working Group Publishes three Updated Reference Catalogs: Catalog of COVID-19 Federal Guidance and References for the U.S. Maritime Industry; Catalog of COVID-19 Best Management Practices for the U.S. Maritime Industry; Catalog of Mental Health Resources for U.S. Merchant Mariners and Critical Workforce in the Marine Transportation System
  • Completed: The final report, The report, The 2021 U.S. Arctic Infrastructure Table Update (April 2021) , an updated inventory of vital marine transportation system (MTS) components in the U.S. Arctic. This 2021 report includes over 40 substantial updates, changes, and additions to the original 2013 infrastructure table and last amendment in 2018.
  • Published updated white papers on importance of U.S. merchant mariners and U.S. port workers to the Nation and accompanying memorandum from RDML Richard Timme: Priority COVID-19 Vaccination of U.S. Port Workers; Priority COVID-19 Vaccination of U.S. Merchant Mariners; Memorandum from Coordinating Board Chair, RDML Timme
  • Published two white papers: Priority COVID-19 Vaccination of U.S. Merchant Mariners and Priority COVID-19 Vaccination of U.S. Port Workers
