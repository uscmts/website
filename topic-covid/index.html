COVID-19 WORKING GROUP

On March 23, 2022, at the request of the CMTS COVID-19 Working Group, the Coordinating Board approved standing down the C19 WG. The work of the WG will be maintained on this website and many of the documents will be permanently available at the National Transportation Library at https://rosap.ntl.bts.gov/welcome. Go to the dedicated CMTS collection button. A number of important issues that were started under C19WG, will be continued under a new Mariner and MTS Workforce Integrated Action Team (Workforce IAT). These include mariner mental health; recruitment and retention; DEI; and military to mariner. Please check back as we build a new page for the Workforce IAT.

Purpose

The COVID-19 Working Group (C-19 WG) serves as the CMTS forum for facilitating high-level interagency discussion, communication, and actions in support of the MTS under and after COVID-19.

Contact Us

MTS stakeholders are welcome to submit their recommendations and questions to the Covid-19 Working Group at C19WG@cmts.gov.

Value

Enhanced interagency discussion and communication regarding the impact on the MTS will ensure that Federal MTS partners are providing the best and most effective support to the MTS under and after the pandemic

  • Facilitate interagency communications and stakeholder engagement
  • Review federal policies, regulatory practices and guidance impacting safe, effective and efficient operation of the MTS under and after the COVID-19 pandemic.
  • Review and provide comments on MTS industry developed COVID-19 Best Practices and Resources.
  • Share and improve resources for addressing U.S. merchant mariner mental health and safety.
  • Assess MTS performance under and after COVID-19 pandemic and develop lessons learned.

Activity and Milestones

  • Conducted public webinar to share results of the 2021 U.S. merchant mariner mental health survey. The webinar slide deck is available here. The survey report by Dr. Marissa Baker, Assistant Professor & Industrial Hygiene Program Director, University of Washington School of Public Health is available at http://deohs.washington.edu/mariner-mental-health.

  • Completed: The final report, The report, The 2021 U.S. Arctic Infrastructure Table Update (April 2021) , an updated inventory of vital marine transportation system (MTS) components in the U.S. Arctic. This 2021 report includes over 40 substantial updates, changes, and additions to the original 2013 infrastructure table and last amendment in 2018.

The CMTS serves as a Federal interagency coordinating committee for the purpose of assessing the adequacy of the marine transportation system, promoting the integration of the marine transportation system with other modes of transportation and other uses of the marine environment, and coordinating, improving the coordination of, and making recommendations with regard to Federal policies that impact the marine transportation system.
